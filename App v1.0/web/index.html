Fracture Types We Detect

Hairline Fracture

Thin, small cracks in the bone, often caused by stress or overuse. Subtle but important to catch early.

Transverse Fracture

Clean break across the bone, usually from direct impact or trauma. Clearly visible on X-rays.

Spiral Fracture

Fracture caused by twisting force, often seen in sports injuries. Has a corkscrew appearance.

Comminuted Fracture

Bone is broken into multiple pieces. Requires detailed analysis and accurate imaging.

Compound Fracture

A fracture where bone breaks through the skin. Urgent and severe — immediate medical attention needed.
